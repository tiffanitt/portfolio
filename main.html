Who I am

I have a wild imagination. I want to bring those ideas to life.

I was in software sales for the majority of my young career, but I always knew I would need to pivot because I was fascinated by software development. A few months ago, I made the decision to take a risk, and to put my career on pause to learn how to code. I wanted something more challenging. I wanted to get out of my comfort zone and challenge myself as much as humanly possible. I do not believe anything is impossible because where there's a will, there's always a way, it really depends on a person's perspective, and the factors involved. I envision a plethora of ideas, but who will be the responsible individual to make these ideas come to life? I wanted to take the lead and be the responsible one so I took a chance.

What I have accomplished since June 2014

I moved to San Francisco in June from Los Angeles to pursue my ambitions of becoming a web developer. I began my journey by studying at RocketU Developer Bootcamp, and now I am building and coding every day to accelerate my progress! This process was in all honesty somewhat painful but 10x more rewarding! As someone who came from a sales background, I struggled to keep up with the pace, struggled to wrap my head around things, struggled like any other human being would who had very little knowledge about coding in general, but that did not stop me from my goals. I see others who had prior experience accelerate ahead of me and that motivated me to learn even more and improve my skills. I know that I have what it takes.
My proudest coding moment: Figuring out the logic of calling APIs. For some it is an easy process, for me, it wasn't as smooth when I was learning it. My solution was by doing a little bit of "market research" with my peers. Asking everyone who knew what they were doing their thought process and eventually I had my own solution as to how APIs worked. I started practicing and building many apps that requires pulling APIs from different sources and calling different data, I overcame my lack of knowledge of APIs in a couple of days. I know there will be many challenges out there for me as a young Junior Web Developer to overcome and I welcome them all with open arms!
